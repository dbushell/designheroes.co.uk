Review

FIELD

Posted by David Bushell

FIELD are a London based agency that "use code to expand the graphical toolbox, and designs custom software to express an idea cross-media."  The studio is led by Marcus Wendt and Vera-Maria Glahn.

New kinds of imagery can be found on unknown pathways. Generative processes, interactivity, and science and technology provide a wide field of inspiration and new ways of thinking that foster unusual strategies and powerful concepts for visual design.

FIELD have produced everything from interactive installations for museums and flagship retail stores, to advertising for the likes of Nokia and AOL. Their portfolio maintains a constant theme of programmatically generated visuals. It's a niche style but one that remains unexplored in design and advertising.

We design custom software tools and processes to express an idea across a wide range of media: from print to animation, interactive installations and websites. Our goal is to merge code-based design with established digital content creation methods. And to create room and connection points for a collaborative and iterative process

What I find most impressive is FIELD's investment in their website. It's an interactive treat coded to web standards (proving again Flash isn't the answer). Each project is put into context with an introduction followed by a full-screen slideshow of imagery and video that works seamlessly with the whole site.

They do an excellent job of documenting their process, research and experiments, positioning themselves at the forefront of code generated design.
