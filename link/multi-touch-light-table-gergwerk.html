Multi Touch Light Table: GERGWERK

Posted by David Bushell

Take a look at this very impressive demonstration of a multi-touch screen with a DJ interface developed by Gergwerk (Gregory Kaufman).

For various reasons (including nostalgia and fear of change) I'd imagine most people would feel uneasy about such a device. The distinct lack of haptic feedback is obviously a questionable feature. What's important is the setup doesn't simply replicate the functionality of a vinyl turntable. While maintaining a similar visual appearance, it uses hand gestures to work the controls. You can see more of the design proces at Behance.

If the iPhone is anything to go by (being the most popular touchscreen device on the market) there is evidence to suggest we are very capable and quick to learn these interfaces. The multitude of different hand gestures make many actions completely invisible. No more dials and switches. That's a bonus for minimalist design, but is it intuitive?

The solution to the problem is to replicate ancestor devices in a visual way. Most iPhone apps have a strong realistic aesthetic, just look at Apple's own iBooks. The interface by Gergwerk's does look like a turntable and that guides the user towards the required actions.

But what about a touch interface that has no real-world equivalent, would the learning curve be too steep? It would seem we are moving closer towards this future so time will tell.
