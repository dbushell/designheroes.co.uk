Review

Unlogo

Posted by David Bushell

Unlogo is a web service that removes brand logos from imagery and videos. See a video introduction and demonstration here: Unlogo Intro on Vimeo.

On a practical level, it takes back your personal media from the corporations and advertisers. On a technical level, it is a really cool combination of some brand new OpenCV and FFMPEG functionality. On a poetic level, it is a tool for focusing on what is important in the record of your life rather than the ubiquitous messages that advertisers want you to focus on.

A small step into the world of augmented reality? I would love to wear a pair of sunglasses with this technology installed.

The Unlogo project was started by Jeff Crouse, a graduate of digital media and fellow at Eyebeam - a not-for-profit art and technology center in New York.
