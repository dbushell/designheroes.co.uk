Review

Grandpeople

Posted by David Bushell

Grandpeople is a Norwegian design agency established in 2005. In the last five years they've been nominated and won numerous awards and have been featured in magazines and books around the world.

We specialize in graphic design, photography, illustration and creative direction. Feel free to have a look at our selected work for clients and collaborators from around the globe.

At the end of June 2010 Grandpeople relaunched their website portfolio. On first glance the website looks like your typical flashy agency website – i.e. 100% unadulterated fullscreen Adobe Flash combining mysterious navigation and five minutes of loading between "pages".

Apologies are of course due as this website isn't Flash. Grandpeople.no is pure HTML using JavaScript Ajax techniques to load and present the design work. The leanness and speed of it makes the site a great experience to browse. It makes all those agencies hiding behind unusable Flash websites look lost in the past.

The photography of Grandpeople's portfolio is outstanding. Sneaking a peak at the JavaScript they even have a resolution independent script that will serve up different size images for optimum performance. It's little unseen features like this that I can appreciate! I'm guessing the website is custom built or maybe a new content management system I'm not aware of? Either way I'd love to see more portfolios built like this.

Since 2005 Grandpeople have worked with a wide range of clients, from big multinationals to start-ups making up for in aspiration what they lack in money. No matter the client, all have shared a desire for singular design and a unique expression for their vision.

The portfolio of Grandpeople is a nice mix of print design and identity. I'll leave you to explore it all but my favourites are Norwegian Rain and Made in Bergen - graphic design at its best!
