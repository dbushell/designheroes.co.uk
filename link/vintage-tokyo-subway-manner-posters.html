Vintage Tokyo Subway Manner Posters

Posted by David Bushell

If you've visited the ever amazing Japanese arts, culture and sci-fi blog Pink Tentacle recently, you would have been treated to these eye-opening Tokyo subway posters.

a few manner posters that appeared in the Tokyo subways between 1976 and 1982

The posters were designed to teach locals and tourists alike what the acceptable manners are, and are not when riding the subway. The poster above is obviously a reminder not to dispose of chewing gum on Superman's boot.

Manners, customs and social norms are certainly a worry for many people in new situations, so I can kind of see the reasoning for such vivid colour and imagery. Anyone who accidentally breaks the "rules" isn't going to appreciate a subtle notice typeset in Helvetica. A picture of Hitler taking up half the bench will get the point across quicker.

I think the London Underground needs a similar set of posters such as "don't stand still staring blanking at the wall in the middle of a jammed corridor". Maybe they already exist I just haven't noticed them...
