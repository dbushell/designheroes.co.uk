Review

Pinterest

Posted by David Bushell

Pinterest is a new social bookmarking website aimed at archiving and sharing things you find interesting online. The site was developed by Cold Brew Labs.

Pinterest is a place to curate and share things you love. Think of it as a virtual pinboard -- a place where you can post collections of things you love, and "follow" collections created by people you admire.

Pinterest is currently in invitation only mode right now but you can request an invite directly, or get friendly with someone on twitter (there's a fair few flying about).

While the concept is nothing new, Pinterest has many useful features and presents them very well. The site provides a JavaScript bookmark that lets you "pin" things you like from other websites. It's very intuitive and easy to use. The ability to create new pinboards and allow other contributors is a nice touch.

Browsing the favourite pinboards give a good overview of who's using the site most. There's a very strong fashion, photography and lifestyle vibe going on at the moment. Contributors (or pinners?) have already embraced Pinterest with great enthusiasm. Inevitably the quality of "pins" will drop, but the concept of following people and pinboards allows you to filter what you see and customize the site to your interests.

I'll be using Pinterest to archive all the random amazing pieces of graphic design and photography I find. Of course, it won't take over Design Heroes unless it becomes addictive! And that just might be possible...

You can follow the development on the Pinterest blog and twitter feed.
